Financially Prepared by Graduation: Financial Checklist for Your College Years

Entering college can be an exciting experience; with newfound freedom, you have more control over your life, but with this comes daunting responsibilities. Of these responsibilities, finances seem to be the most worrying for college students. How do you pay for tuition? Should you save money for the future? When should you start planning for life after graduation?

If you've been experiencing worries regarding your finances, look no further! This financial checklist provides resources and ideas to start your financial literacy journey. Although your life after college may seem far away, it will come up fast, and it's better to be prepared than lost.

1. Create a budget Creating a budget is the first step you should take in starting your financial journey. This is a simple way to understand your monthly needs and expenses so that you can properly allocate your money. Explore the resources we've listed below to get started today!

2. Build an emergency fund If unforeseen costs occur, you can think of an emergency fund as a safety net. Generally, you want to have 3 to 6 months of your monthly costs saved in a savings account. So, if you get sick and can't work, lose your job, your phone stops working, or any other emergencies affecting your daily life, you will have funds to draw from. Having this fund can provide peace of mind when something unexpected happens. Here are some resources explaining an emergency fund and how it should be used. 4. Understand your financial goals Do you want to own a house after college? Do you see yourself running a business? Are kids in your future? Is traveling on your bucket list? While all these questions may seem irrelevant to you, now is the best time to start understanding your financial goals and plan for them. Make a financial date with yourself to understand what you want to accomplish in the next five, ten, or even twenty years. Understanding your goals can allow you to prioritize them and start to save for them so that you can actually achieve them. 6. Start to think about retirement In college, the last thing you're thinking about is retiring. You've got your whole life ahead of you, so why should you think about retirement? Saving for retirement early can mean you can retire when you want and have the funds to do so. This can mean a much more enjoyable and stress-free life because you will be secure in your finances.
